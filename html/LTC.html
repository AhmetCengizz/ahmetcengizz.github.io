What is Litecoin (LTC)?

Litecoin (LTC) is a cryptocurrency designed to provide fast, secure and low-cost payment service by taking advantage of the unique features of blockchain technology. This crypto money developed on the basis of the Bitcoin (BTC) protocol; It differs in the hash algorithm it uses, its maximum supply, block generation time, and a few other factors. Block time of just 2.5 minutes and extremely low transaction fees make Litecoin suitable for micro-scale transactions and point-of-sale payments. Litecoin was released via an open source client on GitHub on October 7, 2011, and the Litecoin network went live five days later, on October 13, 2011. Since then, it has been rapidly adopted among businesses, has been used, and has almost always been among the top ten cryptocurrencies by market capitalization. Former Google employee who developed this cryptocurrency, Charlie Lee, aimed for Litecoin to be "a simpler version of Bitcoin," with similar features to Bitcoin in many respects, but as a lighter alternative.

Why is Litecoin Different?

Litecoin comes after Bitcoin as the second most popular pure cryptocurrency. Behind this success lies its simplicity and benefits of use. Litecoin is one of the most widely used cryptocurrencies as of January 2021, with over 2,000 businesses and stores around the world accepting payments with LTC. Litecoin's main benefit comes from its speed and cost efficiency it provides. Litecoin transactions are usually confirmed within minutes, and transaction fees are almost negligible. In this way, it creates an attractive alternative to Bitcoin, especially in developing countries where transfer fees become the deciding factor in the choice of cryptocurrencies. Litecoin released the MimbleWimble (MW) testnet towards the end of 2020. This network is testing Mimblewimble-based, privacy-focused Litecoin transactions. When this feature is included in the mainnet, Litecoin users will be able to benefit from much stronger privacy and modifiability features.

Where to Buy Litecoin (LTC)

As one of the most common cryptocurrencies, Litecoin is available for sale and trading on a variety of exchanges. Among the most well-known are Huobi Global, Binance, Coinbase Pro, OKEx and Kraken .
