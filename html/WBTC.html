What Is Wrapped Bitcoin [WBTC]?

Wrapped Bitcoin is a tokenized version of the cryptocurrency Bitcoin (BTC) that runs on the Ethereum (ETH) blockchain network. Thanks to its compatibility with WBTC — the main compliance standard of the Ethereum blockchain —  ERC-20, it is fully integrated with ERC-20 supported decentralized finance (DeFi) applications such as decentralized exchange, crypto loan services and predictive markets in the Ethereum ecosystem. Supported at a 1:1 ratio through a network of merchants and custodians that are automatically displayed with Bitcoin, WBTC can thus always fix its price to Bitcoin, while allowing users to transfer liquidity between BTC and ETH networks in a decentralized and autonomous way. Wrapped Bitcoin was first announced on October 26, 2018 and launched on January 31, 2019.

Why is Wrapped Bitcoin Different?

Bitcoin, the oldest and largest cryptocurrency on the market, has a huge user base and a liquidity pool of tens of billions of dollars. However, given modern standards, the blockchain it uses remains relatively simple in function. Utilizing smart contract technology, Ethereum, unlike Bitcoin, was developed to support more advanced usage scenarios from the very first moment, paving the way for the birth of a new industry on its own known as “decentralized finance”. Ethereum and its derivatives provide advanced financial services such as loans and insurance without the need for a secure intermediary. WBTC “wrapping” BTC with the ERC-20 standard ensures the full integration of a Bitcoin-like asset into this advanced environment surrounded by financial decentralized applications, while bringing with it tremendous liquidity tied to the BTC market. Wrapped Bitcoin also significantly simplifies the work of Ethereum-powered exchanges, wallets and payment services: instead of running two separate nodes for ETH and BTC networks, WBTC operations can be supported with a single Ethereum node. Finally, thanks to the Ethereum blockchain's faster average block time—about 15 seconds instead of 10 minutes—the time that WBTC can be transacted is shorter compared to real Bitcoin.
